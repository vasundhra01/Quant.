# Finance
Study of managing, moving, growing money and reducing financial risk  
Capital = money/resource to gen more value  
+ use own profits
+ borrow money
+ raise from investors
Ways to raise Money:
- Debt: borrow, repay with interest
- Equity: sell ownership
#### Stock: reprsents ownership of company
Stock price move because: expected profit/ loss in future, change in demand supply,
news, interest rate, economy etc    
If people believe:  
+ company future = strong → buying increases  
+ company future = weak → selling increases
#### Market Cap = Share Price * Total Shares  
" Value of Company in the stock market "  
### Liquidity
ease of buying/selling
#### Highly liquid: large stocks, cash, ETFs
#### Low liquidity: real estate, rare assets

Bid: Highest price buyer offers.  
Ask: Lowest price seller wants.  
Difference:  Spread=Ask−Bid  
### Index
Tracks group of stocks. NIFTY: tracks 50 major companies in India. if NIFTY rises then overall market is performing well  
#### ETF: Exchange Traded Fund
Basket of assets traded like a stock eg NIFTY ETF and Gold ETF  
#### Bond
Lending money to govt/company. get period interest in return  
High interest rates- borrowing decreases, stock weaken, buy bonds  
Low interest - borrowing inc and stock market rises  
### Risk VS Return
High Risk= High Return
- government bonds → low risk  
- stocks → medium/high risk  
- crypto → very high risk
### Types of Financial Institutions
- Banks: Take deposits and lend money.
- Investment Banks: Help companies: raise capital, IPO, mergers
- Hedge Funds: Use advanced trading/investing strategies.
- Asset Managers: Manage investor money.
- Exchanges: Operate markets.

Traditional Finance: Humans making decisions manually.  
Fintech: Software automating finance. Digital payments, UPI, lending platform, blockchain, trading apps
eg PhonePe, Paytm, Zerodha  
Quant Finance: Math + code + programming + data + statistics driving decisions and solving financial problems.
eg Risk models, pricing options, fraud detection, trading algo  
## Market Mechanics
+ Retail Traders: ecosystem. Groww/Zerodha
+ Institutional Investors Huge organizations: mutual funds, hedge funds, insurance companies. They move massive amounts of money.
+ Market Makers: they cont buy and sell to provide liquidity. Profit from bid-ask spread.  
 Low spread= more liquid market. without MM- market slow, spread widens, trading hard
+ Exchanges: NSE etc. Matches buyers and sellers and maintain order books
+ Brokers: connect trader to exchange. You → broker → exchange.
### Flow
1. You place order on app
2. Broker receives order
3. Broker sends to exchange
4. Exchange finds seller
5. Trade executes
6. Shares transferred to you
7. Money transferred to seller
#### order book:
contains all buy and sell orders -- buyers (bid), sellers (ask). price  
#### bid: highest price buyers offer
#### ask: lowest price seller wants
#### spread= ask-bid  
Market order- Buy immediately at best avail price. Fast execution. no price gurantee  
Limit Order- Buy only at my specific price. Price gurantee. no execution gurantee.    
Matching engine: match buyers and sellers instantly. This is ultra-fast software.  
#### Slippage:
Expected price ≠ actual execution price. That difference is slippage.  
Matters heavig in algorithmic trading, hft and large trades
#### Volume = number of shares/contracts traded.
High volume usually means: strong liquidity, easier trading  
#### Volatility: Measures how rapidly the prices move.
High volatility: larger price swings, higher risk, higher opportunity  
Low volatility: stable prices  

### Clearing
Confirms:
+ buyer obligations
+ seller obligations
### Settlement
Actual transfer:
+ money
+ securities
In India many trades use T+1 settlement( Trade date + 1 business day)
Clearing house acts as middlemen. everyone trusts them. reduces default risk.
Margin: borrowed trading money
Leverage: amplifies profit/loss
Example:  
5% move with 10x leverage: 50% gain OR 50% loss.
#### Arbitrage = profit from price differences.
Example: Gold price differs across exchanges. Buy cheaper. Sell expensive.  
Risk-free arbitrage is rare today because algorithms exploit opportunities quickly.
striking a combination of matching deals to capitalize on the difference, the profit being the difference between the market prices at which the unit is traded.  
#### High Frequency Trading (HFT)
+ HFT firms use:
+ ultra-fast computers
+ low latency systems
+ advanced algorithms
to trade in milliseconds or microseconds.  
Key concept: Speed = advantage.
Latency = delay. Internet delay, server distance, processing speed
#### Market Microstructure
Study of: order flow, liquidity, spreads, execution etc. important for HFT, market making, exchange systems
